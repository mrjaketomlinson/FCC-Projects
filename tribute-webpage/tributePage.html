"The issue today is the same as it has been throughout all history, whether man shall be allowed to govern himself or be ruled by a small elite."

-- Thomas Jefferson

A timeline of some of the major events in Jefferson's public life:

  • 1743 - Thomas Jefferson was born at Shadwell.
  • 1760-62 - Attended the College of William and Mary.
  • 1762 - Began study of law with George Wythe.
  • 1768 - Elected to House of Burgesses. Leveling of Monticello mountaintop for a new home began.
  • 1772 - Married Martha Wayles Skelton. Daughter Martha was born.
  • 1774 - Wrote "A Summary View of the Rights of British America.
  • 1775 - Elected to Continental Congress.
  • 1776 - Drafted Declaration of Independence. Elected to Virginia House of Delegates and appointed to revise Virginia laws.
  • 1779-81 - Served as Governor of Virginia.
  • 1783 - Elected delegate to Congress.
  • 1790-93 - Served as first United States Secretary of State.
  • 1797-1801 - Served as United States Vice President.
  • 1801-09 - Served as United States President.
  • 1803 - Louisiana Purchase concluded. Lewis and Clark expedition launched.
  • 1806 - Lewis and Clark expedition concluded.
  • 1809 - Retired from the presidency and public life.
  • 1815 - Sold 6,700-volume library to Congress.
  • 1825 - University of Virginia opened.
  • 1826 - Died at Monticello, July 4.
